LET'S TALK
For Students & Young Professionals

Nobody teaches this in school. Joe does.

The transition from classroom to career is the biggest bubble most people ever have to burst. School rewards following instructions. Work rewards ownership, initiative, and knowing how to operate around other humans. Joe's early-career program closes that gap.

What the program covers

Built from three decades of watching young professionals succeed and fail, and refined into the Success Skills for Young Adults program, the material covers the things that actually determine early trajectory:

  • How to take ownership of your work before anyone asks you to
  • Getting comfortable being uncomfortable, on purpose
  • How to be coached: taking feedback without folding or bristling
  • Communicating with people who control your next opportunity
  • Building momentum in the first ninety days of any job

Formats

Workshops and group sessions.

For high schools, colleges, athletic programs, and parent groups. Interactive, direct, and built for an audience that can smell a canned speech from a mile away.

One-on-one coaching.

For students and young professionals who want individual attention: internship and job-search strategy, first-job navigation, and building the habits early that most people learn the hard way at forty.

Speaking.

Joe speaks to schools, youth organizations, and businesses onboarding young talent. If your audience is anywhere near the classroom-to-career transition, this is his home turf.

Start with the book

The program's foundation is Joe's book, Bursting the Bubble: A Commonsense Guide to Early Career Success. It's a fast, practical read, and it's the best fifteen dollars a graduate will spend this year.
