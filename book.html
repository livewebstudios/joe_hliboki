The Book

Bursting the Bubble

A Commonsense Guide to Early Career Success

Every student graduates inside a bubble. It's comfortable in there. Instructions are clear, effort gets graded, and someone always tells you what's next. Then the first job starts and the rules change completely, and nobody hands out the new rulebook.

This book is the rulebook.

Drawing on thirty years in enterprise sales and leadership, Joe Hliboki lays out what actually separates young professionals who build momentum from the ones who drift: taking ownership, thinking strategically, getting out of your comfort zone before life drags you out of it. Practical tools. Real stories. Zero corporate filler.

Who it's for

  • High school upperclassmen and college students getting ready for the workforce
  • New graduates in their first job, wondering why school didn't cover any of this
  • Parents who want to hand their kid something more useful than advice
  • Managers onboarding early-career talent

What early readers say

Readers describe it as a fast, practical read that translates decades of workplace experience into plain guidance for the classroom-to-workforce jump, with pointers that land even for readers well past their first job.
